# Category Taxonomy — Chicago Corps

Each category has a definition, inclusion criteria, and examples. A group must match the criteria to be assigned to a category. If a group spans multiple categories, choose the one that best describes its primary mission.

---

## Mutual Aid - General
**Definition:** Organizations whose primary mode is direct, often informal, neighbor-to-neighbor support. They share resources (food, money, supplies), coordinate solidarity networks, and operate on principles of mutual aid rather than charity.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Self-identifies as "mutual aid" OR
- Primary activity is direct resource redistribution among community members AND
- No or minimal bureaucratic intake process

**Examples:** Avondale Mutual Aid, Logan Square Mutual Aid, Westside Mutual Aid

**Excludes:** Traditional nonprofits with formal case management, government programs

**Count:** 22

---

## Food Security
**Definition:** Organizations that address food access through pantries, community fridges, meal distribution, urban agriculture, or food system advocacy.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity is food-related (distribution, growing, or advocacy) AND
- Services are free or sliding-scale

**Examples:** Urban Growers Collective, The Love Fridge Chicago, Food Not Bombs Chicago

**Excludes:** For-profit grocery stores, restaurants (even if community-oriented)

**Count:** 8

---

## Healthcare & Wellness
**Definition:** Organizations providing health services, reproductive care, mental health support, street medic collectives, or health justice advocacy.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity addresses physical or mental health AND
- Services are free, low-cost, or mutual-aid based

**Examples:** Brave Space Alliance, Chicago Abortion Fund, Chicago Action Medical

**Excludes:** Private medical practices, insurance-based clinics

**Count:** 5

---

## Harm Reduction
**Definition:** Organizations focused on reducing harms associated with drug use, sex work, and other stigmatized activities. Includes naloxone distribution, needle exchange, overdose prevention, and drug checking.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity is harm reduction (not abstinence-only or enforcement) AND
- Directly distributes harm reduction supplies or services

**Examples:** Chicago Recovery Alliance, UCHRP, UIC COIP

**Excludes:** Law enforcement drug programs, abstinence-only treatment centers

**Count:** 4

---

## Legal Aid & Community Law
**Definition:** Organizations providing free or pro-bono legal services to low-income residents, with a focus on civil law (housing, immigration, family, employment).

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Provides free legal representation or advice AND
- Serves low-income or marginalized communities

**Examples:** Chicago Volunteer Legal Services, Legal Aid Chicago, Beyond Legal Aid

**Excludes:** Private law firms (even if they do some pro-bono), paid legal services

**Count:** 4

---

## Arts & Culture
**Definition:** Organizations that use arts, culture, and creative practice as vehicles for community building, mutual aid, or social change. Includes community art spaces, cultural centers, and artist cooperatives.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity is arts/cultural programming AND
- Programming is free or low-cost AND
- Has a community-serving (not purely commercial) mission

**Examples:** Chicago Artists Coalition, Hyde Park Art Center, PO Box Collective

**Excludes:** Commercial galleries, for-profit venues

**Count:** 4

---

## Social Justice
**Definition:** Organizations focused on systemic change through political advocacy, anti-racist organizing, community defense, and movement building.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity is political advocacy or systemic change organizing AND
- Explicitly names justice, liberation, or equity in its mission

**Examples:** CAARP, Let Us Breathe Collective, Southside Together

**Excludes:** Electoral campaigns, partisan political organizations

**Count:** 4

---

## Housing Justice
**Definition:** Organizations addressing housing insecurity through tenant organizing, homelessness prevention, eviction defense, or housing policy advocacy.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity is housing-related AND
- Focus is on renter/tenant/unhoused populations

**Examples:** Chicago Housing Justice Coalition, Chicago Tenants Movement, Chicago Coalition to End Homelessness

**Excludes:** Real estate developers, landlord associations

**Count:** 3

---

## Domestic Violence Support
**Definition:** Organizations providing crisis intervention, shelter, legal advocacy, and counseling for survivors of domestic violence and their children.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity is DV survivor support AND
- Services are free and confidential

**Examples:** Between Friends, WINGS Program, Sarah's Inn

**Count:** 3

---

## Migrant & Immigrant Support
**Definition:** Organizations serving immigrant and refugee communities through resettlement assistance, legal aid, language access, citizenship support, and cultural preservation.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary population served is immigrants/refugees AND
- Services address basic needs or legal status

**Examples:** Chinese Mutual Aid Association, ICIRR, MIRA Chicago

**Count:** 3

---

## Community Advocacy
**Definition:** Neighborhood-based organizations focused on local quality-of-life issues, responsive government, and community decision-making.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Geographically bounded to a specific neighborhood or ward AND
- Primary activity is advocacy/organizing around local issues

**Examples:** Bridgeport Alliance, ONE Northside, Southwest Collective

**Excludes:** Citywide advocacy organizations, single-issue campaigns

**Count:** 2

---

## Environmental & Green Space
**Definition:** Organizations focused on environmental education, park stewardship, community gardens, and access to green space.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity is environmental or green-space related AND
- Has a community access or education component

**Examples:** Southside Nature Play, Lake Meadows Park Advisory Council

**Excludes:** Environmental policy organizations (see Environmental Justice)

**Count:** 2

---

## Environmental Justice
**Definition:** Organizations addressing environmental racism, pollution in frontline communities, and climate justice through advocacy and community organizing.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity is environmental advocacy AND
- Focus is on justice/equity for frontline communities

**Examples:** Chicago Environmental Justice Network, 350 Chicago

**Count:** 2

---

## Prison Abolition & Solidarity
**Definition:** Organizations working toward prison abolition, prisoner support, reentry services, and ending systems of state violence.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity addresses incarceration or state violence AND
- Framework is abolitionist or solidarity-based (not reform-only)

**Examples:** MAMAS, Critical Resistance Chicago

**Count:** 2

---

## Youth & Education
**Definition:** Organizations providing after-school programs, youth development, mentoring, and educational enrichment outside the formal school system.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary population served is youth (under 18) AND
- Programs are free or low-cost

**Examples:** Chicago Youth Programs, After School Matters

**Count:** 2

---

## Indigenous Solidarity
**Definition:** Organizations serving Native American, Alaska Native, Native Hawaiian, and First Nations communities in the Chicago area.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary population served is Indigenous AND
- Includes mutual aid or community support programming

**Examples:** Chicago American Indian Community Collaborative, American Indian Center Chicago

**Count:** 2

---

## Community Development
**Definition:** Organizations focused on economic development, neighborhood revitalization, and community building infrastructure.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary activity is community/economic development AND
- Works across multiple issue areas

**Examples:** Westside Community Development Collaboration

**Count:** 1

---

## Worker Co-ops & Economic Justice
**Definition:** Worker-owned cooperatives and organizations building the solidarity economy through cooperative business models.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Legally structured as a cooperative OR
- Primary mission is cooperative economics

**Examples:** Cooperation Racine

**Count:** 1

---

## LGBTQ+ Support
**Definition:** Organizations providing dedicated services, community space, and advocacy for LGBTQ+ people.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary population served is LGBTQ+ AND
- Provides direct services or community space

**Examples:** Center on Halsted (Brave Space Alliance is categorized under Healthcare & Wellness due to its primary service model)

**Count:** 1

---

## Senior & Elder Support
**Definition:** Organizations providing services, social connection, and advocacy for older adults.

**Inclusion criteria:**
- Primary population served is seniors/elders AND
- Includes mutual aid or community-building components

**Examples:** The Village Chicago

**Count:** 1

---

## Category Assignment Rules

1. **Primary mission first.** If a group does multiple things, categorize by what they spend most of their public-facing energy on.

2. **Don't over-split.** A food pantry run by a mutual aid group is still Mutual Aid - General unless food is their entire identity.

3. **When in doubt, prefer specificity.** A trans-led health clinic is Healthcare & Wellness, not LGBTQ+ Support (unless health isn't their primary activity).

4. **Coalitions get their own category based on their stated mission.** Member organizations may have different categories.

5. **If a group truly spans categories equally, pick one and note it in the `notes` field.** Don't create a new category for one edge case.
